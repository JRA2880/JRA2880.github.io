John R. Adams | Technical Game Designer


About Me

A brief introduction about me, and what makes me, well me!

In the beginning, in a galaxy, far, far away...

I have always had a passion for every type of game possible. From playing board games, to tabletop games, and most of all video games, my drive from a young age to one day become a game designer was established early on in life. I have a strong love for all type of games, from First Person Shooters, RTS games, RPG games, and even social games. The most important aspect of games is the experience that either the player or the group of players can experience that can impact them.

How I found my calling...

A mentor of mine once told me, that teachers are really just really bad game designers. From that moment on, I decided to make my lessons like a game. This made my classes engaging, fun, and immersive. It was at this point, that I realized that my lifelong dream is to become a video game designer. I also have a techincal side and a passion for learning programming languages. My first programming language was Python in 2014. From there, I moved on to C# and developing games in Unity. Finally, from my studies in game development, I learned visual scripting and developed games in UE4, C++, and Lua scripting.

Hobbies, interests, and free time...

First and foremost, I love designing and playing games! Whether is it thinking of new campaigns and worlds that will fit in the systems of Dungeons and Dragons, Pathfinder, or the Call of Cthulhu, I love to conceptualize new worlds. When not designing games, I am playing games from a variety of genres to experience new types of gameplay. I feel that it is important to determine what makes a game have a sense of agency and immersion for the player. Finally, I like to play a game of Civ with my friends, going for hikes, or watching the latest sci-fi or fantasy media available.
